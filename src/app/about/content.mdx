# The problem

This idea came when I started a job that was a 1 hour commute from my home. 

You commute every day, but you rarely discover the restaurants and cafes that exist along your route. You either default to the same spots or spend time separately searching for places — without knowing which ones are actually convenient to reach mid-commute.

Ride'N'Dine maps your commute and surfaces food stops that are within a short walk of your transit path, so you can decide before you leave rather than while you're on the bus.

## How to use it

1. **Enter your route** — Type an origin and destination, anywhere in Metro Vancouver. The app works with transit routes (bus, SkyTrain, SeaBus).

2. **Pick a route alternative** — If Google Maps suggests multiple transit options, choose the one that suits you. Each option shows estimated travel time.

1. **Browse restaurants along the way** — The app finds food spots within a 5-minute walk of your transit path. Filter by rating or price, and tap any pin on the map for details.

1. **Open in Google Maps** — Found somewhere you like? Tap the card to open the place directly in Google Maps and get walking directions.

## Usage limits

Each session gets **5 route searches per day**. This keeps the app sustainable during beta — the limit resets at midnight and browsing restaurants on an already-searched route is free.
